📋 Walk-In Script

Follow every step. Don't leave until Step 5 or a hard no.

1
Get to the Decision Maker
"Hi, I'm [Name] with Super Spic & Span. We handle commercial cleaning for businesses in the area. Is the owner or office manager available for just 2 minutes?"
⚡ If unavailable — get their name + best time to call. Do NOT leave without a name.
2
Open Strong
"We're a local cleaning company expanding in this area. I wanted to stop by personally — we're not a franchise, we're local and we actually show up."
⚡ Emphasize LOCAL. People trust it over national chains.
3
Qualify Their Situation
"Do you currently have a cleaning service, or do you handle that in-house?"
⚡ Listen carefully. Either answer has an opening — use it.
4
Handle "We Have Someone"
"Totally understand — honestly most of our clients came from someone else. Is there anything that could be better? We do free walkthroughs. The worst that happens is you have a backup option."
⚡ Never trash the competition. Ask for the walkthrough, not the contract.
5
Ask for the Next Step
"Can I have my manager come do a free walkthrough — no commitment? Would [day] or [day] work?"
⚡ Always give TWO options. Forces a choice, not a yes/no.
6
Leave-Behind Close
"Here's our card. My name's on there — text me directly. I'll follow up [specific day] if I haven't heard back."
⚡ State exactly when you'll follow up. Sets expectation and gives you a reason to call.
💬 Objection Handlers
"We're locked in a contract."
"No problem at all — when does it end? I'll follow up then. Mind if I grab your info so I can check in?"
"We're not interested."
"I totally respect that. Can I at least leave a card in case anything changes? Situations shift."
"We don't have the budget."
"We're very competitive for the area. Can I send a quick quote just so you have a number? Zero pressure."
"The owner makes those decisions."
"Totally — what's the best way to reach them? Or can you pass our info along?"
